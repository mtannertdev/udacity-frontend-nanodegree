Polar Bear

Polar bears aren't white at all, but are transparent. Under intense microscopic imaging their hairs are hollow tubes, without color. This allows them to act like miniature fiber-optic tubes, trapping the warmth of the ultraviolet rays and funneling them back into the bear's body.

  • Scientific Name: Ursus maritimus
  • Conservation status: Vulnerable
  • Average Lifespan: Less than 25 years
  • Primary Diet: Seals

Polar bears are marine mammals, and spend much of their time on Arctic sea ice. Many adaptations make polar bears uniquely suited to life in icy habitats.
